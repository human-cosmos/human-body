# Meditation Practice Guide

Meditation isn't about making the mind blank—it's about letting awareness emerge.

Most people misunderstand meditation: they think it's about "stopping thoughts" or "controlling thinking." But this is exactly the wrong direction—the more you control, the more thoughts multiply.

The correct method: don't control, just watch. Let thoughts flow naturally, let awareness emerge naturally.

## The Essence of Meditation

### Not "Doing," But "Being"

Meditation isn't an activity—it's the natural state after dropping all activities.

Like water surface: you don't need to make water calm, just stop stirring, and water naturally becomes calm.

### Not "Focus," But "Awareness"

Focus locks attention on one point. Awareness lets attention shine evenly like light.

Focus consumes energy. Awareness costs zero energy.

### Not "Pursuing," But "Letting Go"

Pursuing a certain state (calm, emptiness, light) itself is a thought. Drop pursuit, and states naturally appear.

## Preparation

### Time

- **Best times**: After waking, before sleep
- **Duration**: Beginners 15-20 minutes, advanced 30-60 minutes
- **Frequency**: Daily consistency beats occasional long sessions

### Environment

- Quiet space
- Comfortable temperature
- Soft lighting (not too bright)
- Optional: natural sounds, incense

### Posture

**Core principle: Spine upright**

**Sitting options:**

| Posture | Description | Suitable for |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Cross-legged | Full or half lotus | Those with flexibility |
| Chair sitting | Feet on floor | Everyone |
| Elevated sitting | On cushion, hips raised | Those with stiff legs |

**Posture要点:**
- Spine naturally upright, not rigid
- Shoulders relaxed, sinking
- Chin slightly tucked, crown upward
- Hands naturally on knees or abdomen
- Eyes slightly closed or lowered (not fully closed)

## Beginner Meditation: Breath Awareness

### Steps

**Step 1: Settle**

Find a comfortable sitting position. Adjust posture—spine upright, body relaxed. No need for perfect posture, comfort is most important.

**Step 2: Notice Breath**

Place attention gently on breathing. Not staring at breath, but knowing breathing is happening.

- Know air entering nostrils
- Know chest rising and falling
- Know abdomen expanding and contracting

**Step 3: Don't Chase Thoughts**

Thoughts will keep arising. This is normal, not failure.

Key: Don't chase thoughts, don't suppress thoughts.

Like sitting by a river watching water: water flows, you just watch, don't jump in.

**Step 4: Return to Breath**

When you notice being carried away by thoughts, gently return to breath.

Don't criticize yourself ("I wandered again"), just return.

**Step 5: End**

Set a timer. After ending, slowly open eyes, move body.

### Key要点

```
DON'T          DO
─────          ─────
Force focus    Gently know
Control thoughts    Watch thoughts
Pursue emptiness    Drop pursuit
Judge good/bad    Just observe
Expect experiences    Allow happening
```

### Common Issues

**Too many thoughts?**

Normal. Thoughts are the brain's automatic function. Don't fight thoughts, just watch them. With practice, thoughts naturally reduce.

**Physical discomfort?**

Mild discomfort can be observed. Strong discomfort—adjust posture. Don't give up due to discomfort.

**Falling asleep?**

Body needs rest. Can change to lying practice, or choose less relaxing time.

**No experiences?**

Meditation isn't for gaining experiences. Keep practicing, body naturally changes.

## Intermediate Meditation: Awareness Expansion

### From Breath to Whole Body

When breath awareness stabilizes, expand awareness range.

**Steps:**

1. Start from breath awareness
2. Gradually expand awareness to whole body
3. Know body's existence, not focusing on any part
4. Let awareness shine evenly like light over whole body

**Experience:**

- Body becomes a unified presence
- Boundary模糊
- Internal warmth, tingling, fullness sensations

### Body Scanning

Use awareness to scan whole body, finding blockages.

**Steps:**

1. Start from crown
2. Scan down along back of head, cervical spine, back, lumbar
3. Where "can't see" or feels tight
4. Pause there, use awareness to illuminate
5. Continue down to feet

**Effect:**

This uses awareness's high-frequency energy to clear low-frequency knots in fascia. Blockages gradually soften and open.

### Awareness-Thought Relationship

**Observation模式:**

Thought arises → Awareness sees → Thought dissipates → Gap → New thought arises

**Key: Extend gaps**

Gaps are when awareness is clearest. With practice, gaps grow longer.

## Advanced Meditation: Unified Practice

### Calm (Samatha)

"Calm" means settling thoughts, mind stabilizing.

**Method:**
- One thought replacing many (like focusing on breath)
- Brain waves shift from Beta to Alpha, Theta
- Enter deep宁静 state

**Effect:**
- Thoughts reduce
- Body relaxes
- Energy begins flowing

### Insight (Vipassana)

"Insight" means using awareness to observe on calm's foundation.

**Method:**
- Maintain awareness clarity
- Observe all arising phenomena
- Don't judge, don't chase

**Observation objects:**
- Thoughts' arising, persisting, dissipating
- Emotions' flowing
- Body sensations
- Energy flowing

### Unified Practice

Calm and insight aren't separate stages—they occur simultaneously.

```
Calm: Mind stable, thoughts settled
Insight: Awareness clear, observing everything

Together → Unified practice
```

**Effect:**
- Deep宁静 while maintaining clarity
- Clarity while maintaining deep宁静
- Awareness and energy begin merging

## Advanced: Energy Guidance

### Micro-Circuit

When awareness stabilizes, begin experiencing energy flow.

**Method:**

1. Sit, spine upright
2. Focus on lower abdomen (below navel)
3. Deep slow breathing, imagine body as hollow tube
4. Exhale: energy rises from pelvis along spine
5. Inhale: energy descends along front body back to abdomen

**Note:**

- Don't use thought to forcefully push
- Just "watch" energy flowing

- Early stages may not feel明显, keep practicing

**Key要点:**

Mental focus on head = Fire in brain (dangerous)

Correct method: Awareness like pendant lamp hanging above behind head, "shining down" covering brain and body. Is "illumination," not "drilling in."

**Remember:**

## Meditation Mind-State

### Seven Mind-states

1. **Not welcoming** — Thoughts come, don't chase
2. **Not rejecting** — Thoughts come, don't suppress
3. **Not judging** — Don't say good/bad/right/wrong
4. **Not expecting** — Don't expect any experience
5. **Not grasping** — Having experience, don't hold
6. **Not giving up** — No experience, don't discouraged
7. **Just knowing** — Know what's happening now

### Like Cat Watching Mouse Hole

When cat watches mouse hole:
- Doesn't sleep (maintains alert)
- Doesn't move randomly (maintains quiet)
- Doesn't leave (maintains focus)
- But doesn't焦虑 (waiting naturally)

This is meditation's correct mind-state.

## Post-Meditation Transformation

### Integration Period

After meditation ends, don't immediately jump into activity.

**Method:**

1. Maintain a few minutes of quiet
2. Feel energy distribution
3. Let energy naturally settle
4. Slowly move body

**Effect:**

Energy produced by meditation nourishes body, reshapes nervous system and fascia structure.

### Daily Life Continuation

Meditation shouldn't only happen on cushion.

**Continuation methods:**

- Walking—maintain awareness
- Eating—maintain awareness
- Work breaks—return to awareness
- Emotional moments—return to awareness

最终 goal: Awareness becomes 24-hour background state.

## Pitfall Guide

### Common Misconceptions

| Misconception | Correct Understanding |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Meditation stops thoughts | Meditation doesn't chase thoughts |
| Meditation requires focus | Meditation requires awareness |
| Meditation pursues experience | Meditation drops pursuit |
| Meditation is relaxation | Meditation is alert + relaxed |
| Meditation is逃避 | Meditation is facing |

### Danger Signals

**Immediately stop if:**

- Strong dizziness, headache
- Heart beat abnormal
- Persistent fear sensation
- Reality sense loss

**Cause:**

Usually thought过度 active or posture problem.

**Solution:**

Stop practice, return to simple breath awareness, consult professional if needed.
