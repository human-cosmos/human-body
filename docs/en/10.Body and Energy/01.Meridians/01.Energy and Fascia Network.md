## The Anatomy of Meridians

### Fascia Network: The Physical Basis

Fascia is a continuous 3D connective tissue network throughout the body. Meridians aren't separate "pipe" systems—they're low-resistance channels within fascia.

Evidence:
- Meridian routes highly overlap with fascial planes
- Acupuncture points located at fascial layer intersections or muscle boundaries
- Acupuncture stimulation produces sensations propagating along fascial planes

### Piezoelectric Effect

Fascia is rich in collagen—a piezoelectric material. When fascia is stretched or compressed, it generates weak electrical currents.

This means:
- Body movement generates fascial electrical signals
- These signals propagate along fascial networks
- Propagation pathways correspond to "meridians"

### Hydraulic Conduction

Fascial networks are filled with tissue fluid. When one area is pressurized, liquid pressure waves propagate along fascial planes to distant regions.

This explains:
- Why foot pressure can affect the head
- Why leg stretching can relieve back pain
- Why acupuncture at one point can treat distant organs
